Virtual Reality: What its About

Virtual Reality is about the use of computer technology to create a stimulated environment. This includes user experiencing the five senses, vision, hearing, touch, and smell. 3 key takeaways of the VR experience include:

  • Immersion: VR headsets allow the user to fully immerse to another world.
  • Sensory feedback: User moving their heads or bodies will give an illusion of them moving in the virtual world.
  • Interactivity: A stimulated environment should include virutal elements where the user can interact with.

Advantages of using VR

The benefits of VR in the medical field introduce an idea whereby this tool is helpful for both patients and doctors. The first advantage is, virtual reality can elevate the teaching and learning experience in medicine. This means medical students can learn to understand medical concepts better through visual forms. A second advantage is, virtual reality can help relax patients before and after medical procedures. Let's say a patient has a high-risk surgery and is very nervous about it. The use of VR headsets can help distract them even during the surgery. One last advantage is, virtual reality helps to speed up recovery in physical therapy.

Disadvantages of using VR

Going into the disadvantages of VR in the medical field. These include side effects to the cost of virtual reality tools. The first disadvantage is the side effects of virtual reality. Patients can have cybersickness or simulation sickness where they spend too much time in the VR world. Another disadvantage is safety parameters. This means the lack of distinction between the virtual vs. real environment over time. The last advantage there is about using VR, is the cost of it. The specs for the hardware and software of VR can be too expensive.

VR in Sports Field

General Information

VR allows for user's to experience a very immersive scenario, this is no different in the sports world. Atheletes may be in the safety of their own home and still be able to experience the same feeling they get on the field, court, track, or ring. Especially during COVID-19, this has been a driving force in atheletes' lives.

Football

The 3 Main Uses of VR in Football
  • Film
  • Training
  • Concussion Protocols

In the NFL there are cameras everywhere. The advantage of this is that with machine learning, there's software that is able to stitch together all of the camera angles so you're able to get a 360 degree view of plays. These plays can then be played through in a VR headset and players can then replay the exact scenario they were faced with. They can be used to learning moments. In the NFL the difference from the good teams and bad teams are in the way they play.

Through the film provided to them in VR, player's can train near game like conditions in the safety of their home/training facility. If players can elevate their game any way possible then they'll leap at the opportunity.

Chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE) is a big problem in the NFL. CTE is a big problem in the NFL because of the repeated head blows that players face on a regular basis. To combat this, the NFL is always changing concussion protocols in order to protect the safety of the players. Concussion testing has always been a little wishy-washy, but the use of VR may alleviate the pressure. Team doctors may put players through a baseline test in order to gauge what their brain activity is like in certain test scenarios. If a player experience any extreme trauma to the head in game, team doctors will put them through the same test to see if they're fit to keep playing.

Soccer

Some Soccer clubs have already implemented VR in their training regimine. During COVID-19 players were able to practice with each other to reduce the negative effects of isolation. Players would place motion sensors around their living room that would be connected to the VR headsets. Players would also have sensors on their feet/legs that would have haptic feedback when they kicked a ball. Rezzil is a tech company that is leading the charge in this department.

Golf

The 3 Main Uses of VR in Golf
  • Analysis
  • Testing
  • Practice

Golfers are able to run simulations while wearing motion capture suits. The suits will be able to capture the data of a golfer's movements. In golf, even minimal changes in movements may alter the shot path of a swing. In order to combat this, golfers may see analysis of their swings and gain knowledge of what the most optimal swing is and they can just repeat it over and over again.

Golfers may also test different clubs, irons, putters, etc. If they ever think about switching clubs they don't have to go out and buy them. They'll still be holding these clubs in their hands, they just won't be swinging at a physical ball which may damage the club.

Not everyone has the privilege of living in perfect conditions for a course. There is now the option of playing on real golf courses in the safety of your home. These simulations with prove to be nearly identical to the real life courses.

Main Takeaways
  • Protects the safety of players

  • No substitute for actual game experience
  • Requires significant financial investments from user's
